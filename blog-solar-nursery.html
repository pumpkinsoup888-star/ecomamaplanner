☀️ Solar Nursery

Solar-Powered Nursery
Products That Save You Money

Eco-friendly solar and low-energy nursery products that reduce your electricity bill and keep baby safe.

📅 March 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 🌿 EcoMamaPlanner

Setting up a nursery is exciting — but it can also be surprisingly energy-intensive. Night lights, baby monitors, sound machines, fans, bottle warmers — the list of electrical gadgets recommended for new parents keeps growing. Here's how to create a beautiful, functional nursery that runs on as little electricity as possible, saves you money every month, and keeps harmful electromagnetic fields (EMFs) away from your sleeping baby.

"A solar-powered nursery isn't just better for the planet — it's better for your baby's health and your household budget."

Solar night lights

Nursery night lights are on for hours every night. A solar-powered night light charges during the day on a windowsill and runs all night for free — zero electricity cost, zero cords near baby. Look for lights with a warm amber glow (cooler blue-toned lights can disrupt both your and your baby's sleep hormones).

Low-EMF baby monitors

Conventional wifi baby monitors emit continuous radiofrequency radiation — and they sit centimetres from your sleeping baby's head. Consider switching to a DECT baby monitor (which only transmits when sound is detected) or a wired video monitor (which uses ethernet instead of wifi). Both dramatically reduce your baby's EMF exposure during the long hours of sleep.

Solar-powered outdoor products

If you have a garden or balcony, solar-powered fairy lights create a magical space for tummy time and outdoor play — completely free to run. Solar garden lights, solar-powered baby swings for the garden, and solar water features for white noise are all wonderful eco additions to your outdoor space.

Energy-efficient white noise machines

White noise machines are one of the most helpful baby sleep tools — but they can run all night, every night, for months. Look for models with a sleep timer or low-power mode. Alternatively, a simple USB-powered fan uses minimal electricity and provides both white noise and airflow — important for safe sleep.

Rechargeable baby gear

Many battery-powered baby products — bouncy chairs, swings, mobiles, and toys — go through batteries at a remarkable rate. Switching to rechargeable batteries (or rechargeable-via-USB versions of these products) reduces both waste and ongoing cost significantly.

LED lighting throughout the nursery

If you haven't already switched to LED bulbs throughout your home, the nursery is a great place to start. LED bulbs use up to 90% less electricity than incandescent bulbs and last for years. Choose bulbs with a warm colour temperature (2700K–3000K) for a cosy nursery atmosphere that's gentle on developing eyes.

The bigger picture

Every kilowatt-hour of electricity you save in the nursery is one less unit of fossil fuel burned — and a few dollars back in your pocket each month. Over the three years your child might be in a nursery, these small savings add up to a meaningful amount. And the habit of choosing low-energy products tends to spread throughout the whole home, multiplying the benefit many times over.
