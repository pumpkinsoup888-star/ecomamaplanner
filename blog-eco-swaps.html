🌿 Eco Pregnancy

10 Eco-Friendly Swaps Every
Expecting Mama Should Make

Simple swaps to reduce toxins, save money, and protect your baby before they even arrive.

📅 March 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 🌿 EcoMamaPlanner

Becoming a mama is one of the most powerful motivators to live more consciously. Suddenly, the ingredients on the back of a shampoo bottle matter. The chemicals in your cleaning products matter. The plastic in your food containers matters. The good news? You don't have to overhaul everything at once. These 10 swaps are easy, affordable, and make a real difference.

"You don't have to be perfect — you just have to start. Every swap is a step toward a healthier home for your baby."

In the bathroom

1. Swap your body wash for a natural alternative

Conventional body washes are often loaded with synthetic fragrance, parabens, and sulphates. During pregnancy, your skin absorbs more than usual. Switch to an organic castile soap or a fragrance-free natural body wash. Brands like Dr. Bronner's are gentle, affordable, and widely available.

2. Replace your deodorant

Many conventional deodorants contain aluminium compounds, which some studies suggest may be worth avoiding during pregnancy. Natural deodorants using magnesium, baking soda, or crystal salts work well for most mamas. Give your body a few weeks to adjust if you're switching.

3. Switch to a mineral sunscreen

Chemical sunscreens (containing oxybenzone and octinoxate) are best avoided during pregnancy. Switch to a mineral sunscreen with zinc oxide or titanium dioxide — these sit on top of the skin rather than being absorbed, making them a safer choice for you and your baby.

In the kitchen

4. Ditch plastic food containers

Even BPA-free plastics can leach chemicals when heated. Replace plastic containers with glass, stainless steel, or ceramic alternatives. This is especially important for food that goes in the microwave or contains acidic ingredients like tomatoes or citrus.

5. Filter your drinking water

Tap water in many areas contains chlorine, fluoride, heavy metals, and trace pharmaceuticals. A simple carbon filter jug or under-sink filter removes the majority of these contaminants. Carry your water in a glass or stainless steel bottle to avoid plastic leaching.

6. Buy the dirty dozen organic

You don't need to buy everything organic — focus on the EWG's Dirty Dozen: the 12 most pesticide-contaminated fruits and vegetables. These include strawberries, spinach, apples, grapes, and peaches. Switching just these 12 to organic reduces your pesticide exposure significantly.

In the laundry room

7. Switch to fragrance-free laundry detergent

Synthetic fragrance is one of the most common hidden allergens in home products. Switch to a fragrance-free, plant-based laundry detergent for all baby clothes and bedding. Brands like Ecover, Method, and Seventh Generation are effective and eco-friendly.

8. Stop using dryer sheets

Conventional dryer sheets coat fabrics with synthetic chemicals that stay on your skin all day. Replace them with wool dryer balls — they naturally soften clothes, reduce drying time, and last for years. Add a few drops of lavender essential oil to the balls for a gentle natural scent.

In the nursery

9. Choose a low-VOC paint

VOCs (volatile organic compounds) in conventional paint off-gas for months after application. When painting your nursery, choose a zero-VOC or low-VOC paint. Your baby will spend up to 18 hours a day in that room — the air quality matters enormously.

10. Invest in an organic cot mattress

Your baby will spend more time on their mattress than anywhere else. Conventional mattresses are often made with polyurethane foam, fire retardants, and waterproofing chemicals. An organic cotton or natural latex mattress is free from these chemicals and is one of the most impactful swaps you can make.
