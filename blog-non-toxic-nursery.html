🏡 Eco Nursery

How to Set Up a Non-Toxic
Nursery on a Budget

Create a safe, beautiful nursery free from harmful chemicals — without spending a fortune.

📅 March 2026 ⏱ 6 min read 🌿 EcoMamaPlanner

Your baby will spend up to 18 hours a day in their nursery for the first year of their life. The air they breathe, the surfaces they touch, and the materials surrounding them have a direct impact on their developing immune system, hormones, and neurological health. Setting up a non-toxic nursery doesn't require an unlimited budget — it requires smart choices in the right places.

"The nursery is where your baby will spend most of their first year. It's worth making it the safest room in the house."

Start with the paint

Conventional paint contains volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that off-gas for months — sometimes years — after application. These airborne chemicals are particularly harmful to developing lungs and nervous systems. Always choose a zero-VOC or low-VOC paint for the nursery. Paint the room at least 8 weeks before your due date with windows open, and air it out thoroughly.

The most important purchase: the mattress

Your baby's cot mattress is the single most important eco purchase in the nursery. Conventional mattresses are made with polyurethane foam (derived from petrochemicals), fire retardants containing phosphorus or bromide compounds, and waterproofing chemicals. Your baby's face will be pressed against this surface for years.

Choose a mattress made from natural latex, organic wool, or organic cotton. Natural wool is naturally fire-resistant, eliminating the need for chemical fire retardants. Look for certifications like GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard) or OEKO-TEX. Yes, organic mattresses cost more — but this is the one place in the nursery where it's absolutely worth spending the extra money.

Furniture: solid wood only

Many affordable nursery furniture pieces are made from MDF or particle board — materials that contain formaldehyde-based adhesives that off-gas continuously. Choose furniture made from solid wood wherever possible, especially for the cot and changing table. Second-hand solid wood furniture is an excellent option — older pieces have already off-gassed most of their VOCs.

Flooring and rugs

Wall-to-wall carpet in nurseries traps dust mites, VOCs, and allergens. Hard flooring (timber, bamboo, or cork) is a healthier choice. If you want softness and warmth, add a natural fibre rug — wool, cotton, or jute — rather than a synthetic carpet. Avoid rugs with stain-resistant treatments, which typically contain PFAS (forever chemicals).

Soft furnishings and textiles

Choose GOTS-certified organic cotton for all nursery textiles — sheets, blankets, swaddles, and cushion covers. Organic cotton is grown without pesticides, processed without harsh chemicals, and is gentler on sensitive newborn skin. It's also more durable than conventional cotton, lasting through multiple children.

Cleaning the nursery

Once you've set up a non-toxic nursery, keep it that way. Clean surfaces with a simple solution of diluted white vinegar or a fragrance-free plant-based cleaner. Avoid aerosol sprays, air fresheners, and scented candles in the nursery — these all introduce airborne chemicals into a space where your baby breathes deeply for hours at a time.
