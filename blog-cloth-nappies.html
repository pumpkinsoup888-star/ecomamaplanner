🌿 Eco Pregnancy

The Complete Guide to
Cloth Nappies

Everything you need to know about cloth nappies — costs, types, washing routines, and how to get started.

📅 March 2026 ⏱ 7 min read 🌿 EcoMamaPlanner

A baby in disposable nappies generates approximately 5,000 nappies worth of landfill before they're toilet trained. Each one takes up to 500 years to decompose. Cloth nappies are one of the single most impactful eco choices you can make as a parent — and they've come a long way from the terry squares of your grandparents' generation. Modern cloth nappies are easy, effective, and genuinely enjoyable to use.

"Switching to cloth nappies is one of the biggest eco wins available to any family — and the savings are extraordinary."

The financial case for cloth nappies

A full set of cloth nappies typically costs $300–$600 upfront for 20–25 nappies. Disposables, by contrast, cost an average of $2,500–$4,000 per child over their nappy-wearing years. If you use the same cloth nappies for a second child, the savings double. Even accounting for water and electricity for washing, cloth nappies are dramatically cheaper over time.

Types of cloth nappies

All-in-ones (AIOs)

The simplest option — everything is sewn together, just like a disposable but reusable. Great for daycare and caregivers who aren't familiar with cloth. Slightly slower to dry because all layers dry together.

Pocket nappies

A waterproof outer with a pocket that you stuff with absorbent inserts. Quick to dry, very adjustable for absorbency, and popular with most cloth nappy families. The most versatile option for beginners.

Fitted nappies with covers

A fully absorbent fitted nappy worn under a separate waterproof cover. The most absorbent option — excellent for heavy wetters and overnight use.

Flat and prefold nappies

The most affordable and eco-friendly option. Single-layer fabric that you fold yourself, secured with a snappi or pins, worn under a waterproof cover. Takes a little practice but dries fastest and lasts longest.

How many do you need?

For a newborn, you'll need to wash every 1–2 days. A stash of 20–25 nappies is ideal to start. For older babies (6+ months) who wee less frequently, 15–20 nappies is usually sufficient.

The washing routine

The thought of washing nappies puts many parents off, but the reality is simple. A two-step wash routine works well for most families:

  • Pre-wash: a short, warm cycle (40°C) to rinse out waste
  • Main wash: a longer cycle (60°C) with a good detergent to fully clean and sanitise

Dry in the sun when possible — UV light naturally sanitises and removes stains. Avoid fabric softener (it reduces absorbency) and strong stain removers (they degrade the waterproofing).

Getting started

Don't feel like you need to go all-in immediately. Many families start with cloth part-time — at home during the day — and gradually increase as they get comfortable. Buying a few second-hand nappies to trial before investing in a full stash is a smart approach. Local cloth nappy libraries often lend trial kits for free.
