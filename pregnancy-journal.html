Why journal during pregnancy

The benefits of a pregnancy journal

🧠

Process your emotions

Pregnancy brings a flood of emotions — excitement, fear, joy, anxiety. Writing them down helps you process them, reducing anxiety and improving emotional wellbeing. Research shows that expressive writing during pregnancy is associated with lower rates of postnatal depression.

💝

Bond with your baby

Writing letters to your baby and reflecting on their development each week deepens your sense of connection before they're even born. This prenatal bonding is beneficial for both mama and baby — and the letters become a priceless keepsake.

📖

Preserve your memories

Pregnancy moves fast — the details blur quickly. A pregnancy journal preserves those precious, fleeting details: how it felt to hear the heartbeat for the first time, the exact moment you felt the first kick, your hopes and dreams in those weeks of anticipation.

🌿

Practise mindfulness

Regular journaling encourages you to slow down and be present in your pregnancy. In a culture that rushes through everything, deliberately pausing to reflect on this experience — even for 10 minutes a week — is a powerful act of self-care.

👩‍⚕️

Track symptoms and questions

Writing down symptoms, concerns, and questions between appointments means you never forget to mention something important to your midwife or doctor. Many mamas find they arrive at appointments better prepared and more confident when they've been keeping notes.

🎁

A gift for your child

Imagine your child reading your letters to them one day — learning about the world that greeted them before they arrived, feeling the love you had for them before you'd even met. A pregnancy journal is one of the most meaningful gifts you can give your child.
